How to calibrate

  1. Pick a straight reference on flat ground: a tape measure or rope stretched taut works best. Aim for it to span a wide angle — roughly the size of what you'll actually check (for a 120° cone, that means long: 8–15 m is a good target, not something small and close).
  2. Measure its exact length → that's Width.
  3. Stand back facing its midpoint square-on (not at an angle), phone held level → measure that distance → that's Distance.
  4. Enter both numbers, then drag the cyan (L) and orange (R) lines onto the two ends of the reference on screen.
  5. Tap Compute, sanity-check the result, then Save.

Calibrate with the same lens (camera switch button) you'll use for the real check afterward — switching lenses invalidates the calibration.
